Design Comp Summary

For the second version of this design comp, I tried to refine my choice to make the page more readable and accessible. I refined my color scheme and changed the text to an off-black color instead of solid black to make the contrast less harsh and easier on the eyes. I also adjusted the shades of green used to try to make a more balanced composition. In addition, I made sure to avoid using red in the color scheme in order to maintain accessibility for people with red-green colorblindness.

As a result of my work in studio 4, I added another page to the site for maintaining a grocery list where the items can be compared. I believe this will add more useful functionality to the design, and I am considering removing the label guide page in order to make sure the site aligns more precisely with its purpose.

In terms of layout, for the most part I spread out the elements and added more spacing around the text to make it feel less cramped. I added some elements to the homepage to make it easier to browse items, rather than depending only on the search, which may be an unclear type of interaction. In addition, I added arrows to go back from the single item page and a button to add it to the grocery list. I plan to have popups or tooltips on hover to make their functions clearer. I also changed the location of the alternative items to the bottom on the page to avoid confusion.

Overall, I tried to keep the interface simple and hopefully easy to understand.

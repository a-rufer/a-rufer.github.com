Learn the story of your produce:

[x]

First, the carrot must be grown.

This has a cost in labor, water, and CO2 from machines used in the growing process.

Some foods have higher cost than others. For example, almonds take 8047 m^3/ton water to grow, while carrots take 195 m^3/ton.

Then, the carrots are transported from the farm to the store.

If carrots are locally out of season, this increases the distance traveled by the carrot, since it must be transported from an area which has them in season.

If the carrot is put in storage, there are costs of maintaining it in good condition, such as cooling.
